Surrounded by the ocean of praises, only to get drowned by the strong wave from friends and family members who are "PROFESSIONAL HYPIST". They incessantly chant at you all sort of accolades:"You look like a rich man", "You sound like a great orator", "You write like a great man", "Scholar", "Boss!"... Nice compliments but based on ridiculous, and often meaningless impulse.


It is imperative to establish that, it is one thing to "look like" and the other to "be Like". If one must settle for any, don't just choose to "look-like" or wish to just "be like" anything or someone, choose rather to become a true version of one own self - not a photocopy or look-like.

Judging by the recent trends on social media, and the uproars of distracting elements,
one insidious virus one must always stay alert to, is the "Hype Virus". The word "hype" or "whine" as colloquially referred to means "a promotion or propaganda, usually and exaggerated claims". Virus, as used in this context, means a deadly disease.

While I was in secondary, the dullest person in class then, who could not write A-Z completely but always stops at letter "P" was ironically called "Prof". Even in the university today, everyone is a scholar, both those with a prr C.G.P.A almost at the verge of of been withdrawn, we still call them "scholar!".

Also, I find my self in church, and one would see a young man who cannot quote a single scripture of the bible except John 3:16 
been addressed as a an Apostle or a Bishop - interesting.

Where are we heading to? Are we really helping matters? One thing that we must all do is to learn to filter out and discard the hype statement and sentiments.  Always evaluate yourself to see if the praises been hauled at you equate who you really are. If it does or not, do not give room to complacency.

Many destiny have been destroyed. Many are drowning in the ocean of sycophantic and superfluous praises. Many have broken down at the border of breakthrough. All because of "HYPE".

The latest trend now is Commercial hyping. People hyping, so as to extort money or get some personal benefit from an individual. We have professional Hypemen. Hmmmmmmmm...
 
This is not to say that we shouldn't appreciate or encourage people when they do great things, or we shouldn't get jovial with our colleagues, partners, and those around us. This is only to say that compliments must be free from deceit or propaganda that often lead people to 
fall, become complacent, lead to delusion of grandeur or even pressure and depression to keep.

Lest I forget, it is imperative for one to have a mentor that one may appear to "look like", but while one follows one mentor, one must first discover one's unique purpose in life.

The journey of self discovery and mastery of oneself coupled with consistency would take you to your desired "SPACE".
